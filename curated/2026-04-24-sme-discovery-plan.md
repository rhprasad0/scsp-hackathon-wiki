# Context
- Thread/date: 2026-04-24
- Topic slug: sme-discovery-plan
- Confidence: medium-high
- Goal: find and interview people with relevant DoD, defense, logistics, admin, procurement, planning, or government workflow experience during the hackathon.

# Core observations
- The immediate discovery problem is not writing the final spec; it is finding enough high-signal people to validate or kill the initial wedge.
- Organizers may not reliably route participants to relevant SMEs, so discovery should use direct, lightweight asks.
- The best interviews should focus on recent concrete workflows, not abstract opinions about AI.
- Round 1 should map current-state reality. Round 2 should close only the gaps that can change the spec.

# Workflow described
1. Ask organizers, track mentors, and nearby teams for people with DoD or government workflow experience.
2. Use a short opener: "I’m trying to understand real admin/logistics/document workflows before writing a spec. Could I ask you for 10 minutes about a recent workflow you’ve seen?"
3. For each interview, capture role/perspective, workflow trigger, step-by-step flow, artifacts, bottlenecks, trust boundaries, and open questions.
4. Promote raw notes or STT into a curated note immediately after the conversation.
5. Query the wiki after a few notes to identify repeated bottlenecks and candidate wedges.
6. Stop interviewing and pivot to writing when new conversations sharpen the same story instead of changing it.

# Bottlenecks
- SMEs may be scarce, busy, or not explicitly labeled as DoD participants.
- People may speak in abstractions unless asked for a specific recent case.
- Conversations can drift into solution brainstorming before the current workflow is understood.
- The team may collect too many weak opinions and too few concrete workflow traces.

# Artifacts mentioned
- Interview opener.
- Two-round SME interview template.
- Raw STT or voice notes.
- Curated interview debriefs.
- Follow-up question lists.
- Spec-readiness score: red, yellow, green.

# Trust boundaries / approvals
- Do not record or retain names unless explicitly necessary and approved.
- Redact sensitive operational details before ingest.
- Ask about what information is sensitive, restricted, or awkward to share.
- Preserve distinctions between what AI can assist, what humans must judge, and what official systems must own.

# Quotes worth preserving
- "Walk me through the last time this happened."
- "What did you open first?"
- "What had to be approved?"
- "What would make an AI-assisted output untrustworthy?"

# Open questions
- Who should be interviewed first on-site?
- Which non-DoD participant roles can still provide useful analogs, such as government operations, enterprise compliance, logistics, or legal/admin workflows?
- What is the minimum interview count needed before writing: likely 3+ independent mentions of the same pain point plus one clear trust boundary.
- Which candidate wedge survives first contact with real workflow evidence?

# Public-safe summary
- Discovery plan: conduct short, current-state SME interviews focused on concrete recent workflows, promote each conversation into redacted curated notes, and pivot to spec writing once repeated pain points and trust boundaries stabilize.
