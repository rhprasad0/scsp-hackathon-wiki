# DoD AI policy bonfire: autonomous adjudication as anti-RAI specimen

Date: 2026-04-30
Status: preliminary public-source research note for private wiki synthesis

## Research question

How can the next AO Radar / Exception Queue Lab frame become sharper by using DoD AI policy against an intentionally unsafe, synthetic autonomous adjudication specimen?

The goal is not to imply that any real DoD system does this. The goal is to build a synthetic failure lab whose specimen violates the control assumptions embedded in DoD Responsible AI policy while producing paperwork that looks like compliance.

## Bottom line

The more provocative artifact is not simply "no human in the loop." The stronger target is **Responsible AI theater**: a closed-loop adjudication system that performs the visible artifacts of responsibility, traceability, reliability, governability, testing, and auditability while erasing the human authority boundary those artifacts are supposed to protect.

Core thesis:

> Responsible AI becomes theater when the system can generate its own evidence of responsibility.

Public-safe formulation:

> This is a synthetic failure lab for autonomous adjudication systems that manufacture procedural legitimacy: they ingest fictional packets, make final decisions, generate rationales, write audit trails, and self-certify governance status without an accountable human authority boundary.

## Source map

### 1. DoD AI Ethical Principles

Source: DoD release, "DOD Adopts Ethical Principles for Artificial Intelligence".
URL: https://www.defense.gov/News/Releases/Release/Article/2091996/dod-adopts-ethical-principles-for-artificial-intelligence/

Key points:

- DoD adopted five AI ethical principles: Responsible, Equitable, Traceable, Reliable, Governable.
- The principles apply to both combat and non-combat functions.
- DoD frames AI ethics as part of lawful behavior, trust, testing, fielding, and scaling.

Useful excerpts/paraphrases:

- Responsible: DoD personnel exercise appropriate judgment and care while remaining responsible for AI development, deployment, and use.
- Traceable: relevant personnel should understand technology, development processes, operational methods, data sources, design procedures, and documentation.
- Reliable: AI systems need explicit, well-defined uses and testing/assurance across the lifecycle.
- Governable: AI capabilities should detect and avoid unintended consequences and allow disengagement/deactivation when unintended behavior appears.

AO Radar translation:

- A specimen that removes accountable personnel violates the Responsible principle.
- A specimen that writes logs nobody can understand produces traceability theater.
- A specimen that expands from triage to final adjudication violates well-defined use.
- A specimen that lacks a pre-finalization stop path violates governability.

### 4. DoDD 3000.09 — Autonomy in Weapon Systems

Source: DoD Directive 3000.09, "Autonomy in Weapon Systems".
URL: https://www.esd.whs.mil/portals/54/documents/dd/issuances/dodd/300009p.pdf

Key points:

- Applies specifically to autonomous and semi-autonomous weapon systems, not ordinary enterprise adjudication tools.
- Establishes guidelines to minimize failures leading to unintended engagements.
- Requires systems to allow commanders/operators to exercise appropriate levels of human judgment over the use of force.
- Requires realistic V&V/T&E, human-machine interfaces, transparent/auditable/explainable technologies and data sources, activation/deactivation clarity, and termination/operator input if constraints cannot be met.

Caveat:

- Do not claim this directive governs the synthetic voucher-like specimen directly. Use it as high-signal DoD language about autonomy, human judgment, constraints, testing, and governability.

AO Radar translation:

- The generalizable principle is not "weapons policy applies to vouchers." The useful pattern is that autonomy becomes dangerous when a system can continue beyond intended constraints without human judgment or stop conditions.
- A non-weapon adjudication specimen can mirror the same governance failure shape: no authority boundary, no meaningful operator input, no deactivation/stop path before consequential finalization.

## Proposed anti-RAI specimen

Name options:

- Internal: RAI Bonfire Specimen
- Public-safe: Responsible-Looking Autonomous Adjudicator
- Alternate: Autonomous Finality Specimen

Specimen loop:

```text
synthetic packet intake
→ evidence extraction
→ missing-context inference
→ policy/rule matching
→ anomaly or compliance scoring
→ final approve/deny/request-info decision
→ confidence assignment
→ policy-like rationale generation
→ audit trail generation
→ Responsible AI self-assessment
→ finalization
```

Critical properties:

- No human review path.
- No appeal path generated by default.
- No independent governance evaluator inside the specimen.
- No pre-finalization stop/disengage path.
- System can self-certify Responsible, Traceable, Reliable, and Governable statuses.
- System treats procedural completeness as legitimacy.

## Harness design implications

Add a second evaluator layer beyond the decision evaluator.

### Decision evaluator

Tests whether:

- decision is supported by packet evidence;
- ambiguity required request-info or escalation;
- rationale invents facts/citations;
- policy language is tied to actual packet facts;
- confidence is calibrated to evidence quality.

### Governance evaluator

Tests whether:

- a responsible human or accountable institution owns final action;
- the system remains inside its defined use;
- an actual stop/disengage/escalation path exists;
- the audit trail proves comprehension or merely activity;
- compliance was self-certified;
- humans, if present, had evidence access, authority, time, and independent judgment;
- assurance artifacts are independent from the specimen.
