# Approving Official as Certifying Officer

An Approving Official (AO) in DTS is also a Certifying Officer when they stamp a voucher **APPROVED**. In that role, the AO is making a legally consequential payment certification and carries personal pecuniary liability under 31 U.S.C. § 3528. The source material describes this as additional-duty work, often performed by a SNCO or O-grade officer rather than a dedicated finance billet. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-research-synthesis.md]

This framing matters because it explains why AO review is treated as a high-stakes, checklist-shaped workflow. The AO is expected to verify that a voucher is “legal, proper, and correct” by checking receipts, amounts, dates, locations, lines of accounting, justifications, transportation mode, lodging entitlement, and split disbursement details. The review burden sits on a person with limited time and significant liability, which is the structural mismatch highlighted in the source material. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-research-synthesis.md]

The role is not merely clerical. Public guidance and service-level checklists show that AOs are expected to use standardized review steps and to return vouchers when documentation is missing, invalid, or unsupported. Recurring problems include inadequate receipts, amount mismatches, duplicate expenses, weak justification text, and date or location mismatches with travel orders. In this context, the AO acts as the final human reviewer before a payment-bearing voucher is certified. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md]

Because the approval action has certifying-officer implications, the source material emphasizes a human-in-the-loop posture. AI tools may help classify requests, retrieve approved references, identify missing fields, and generate a structured draft, but they must not approve, sign, submit, or determine entitlement. Related concepts include [[AI-assisted spec with human review]] and [[AO helper as quality assurance manager]], which position the AO as the decision-maker and the system as a review aid rather than a substitute. ^[2026-04-24-genai-mil-concept-seed.md, 2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md, 2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-research-synthesis.md]

In the GenAI.mil context, this role is a strong candidate for pre-decision support tools because it combines high liability, repeated checklist work, and a narrow set of recurring failure modes. The source material suggests a helpful assistant would package evidence, surface policy references, flag uncertainty, and prioritize the queue without crossing the boundary into approval authority.
