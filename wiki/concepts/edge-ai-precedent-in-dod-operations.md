# Edge AI precedent in DoD operations

Edge AI precedent in DoD operations refers to public examples showing the Department of Defense using AI at the edge or in denied, degraded, intermittent, and limited (DDIL) environments rather than only through a centralized cloud platform. In the GenAI.mil context, this precedent matters because the track’s “offline” wording can be read as a call for on-device or disconnected operation, which aligns with existing DoD edge-AI investment and with use cases that do not depend on persistent cloud connectivity. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

One cited precedent is Google Distributed Cloud air-gapped appliance work demonstrated at U.S. Air Force Mobility Guardian 2025 in Guam. The public context note describes this as disconnected IL2 collaboration with on-device generative AI for transcription, OCR, translation, and summarization, with sync-back when connectivity is restored. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

Another precedent is EdgeRunner AI, described as an on-device, air-gapped system for DoD users. The note says EdgeRunner announced a public beta for DoW users in September 2025, followed by dual CRADAs with the Army Research Laboratory and Air Force Research Laboratory in October 2025; it also highlights support for RAG, text-to-speech, and speech-to-text, along with a smaller model footprint and near-retained quality. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

A third precedent is Legion Intelligence’s Centurion, which the source material describes as DDIL-targeted edge AI for the U.S. Army’s 18th Airborne Corps. The public context note characterizes it as an offline-first agentic workflow system with mesh sync, announced in January 2026 and positioned for deployable edge use. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

Taken together, these examples suggest that an effective GenAI.mil submission does not need to be a cloud-scale chatbot to be relevant. The public context argues that a narrow assistant with a baked-in public DoD doctrine corpus, no internet round-trip, and local retrieval or drafting would be consistent with current edge-AI precedent, while still staying within the administrative and back-office problem space emphasized by the track.
