# [[Approving Official as Certifying Officer]] with pecuniary liability

An Approving Official (AO) in the Defense Travel System (DTS) is the official who applies the APPROVED stamp to a travel authorization or voucher. When that approval causes a payment, the AO is acting as a Certifying Officer (CO) under 31 U.S.C. §§ 3325 and 3528, and the human role carries personal pecuniary liability for erroneous payments. AOs must also have a written DD Form 577 appointment and complete Certifying Officer Legislation training before serving and annually thereafter. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md]

The AO role is not a standalone finance billet in most cases. Public service guidance describes it as an additional duty performed within the operational chain, and the source material emphasizes that AOs cannot approve their own travel payments except under narrow self-authorizing exceptions that do not extend to certifying their own claims for payment. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md]

Because the AO is personally exposed to pecuniary liability, DTS voucher review is structured as a control process rather than a simple formality. DTMO guidance instructs AOs to use a standardized checklist or process and to verify trip dates, locations, travel mode, supporting records, receipts, pre-audit flags, reason codes, advisories, and the validity of the document before approval. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md]

Public guidance and service-level checklists show that the AO’s review includes recurring problem areas such as missing or invalid receipts, duplicate expenses, amount mismatches, weak justifications, DD Form 1351-2 issues, and date/location mismatches between the voucher and the underlying orders. The Navy’s mandatory checklist is especially explicit about checking for duplicate expenses, itemized receipts, proper reason codes, and meaningful justification text rather than placeholder entries. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md, 2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md]

The accountability burden is reinforced by oversight findings. DoD OIG and GAO reports describe persistent travel-pay error rates and note that vouchers were approved without required receipts when approving officials could not determine whether documentation had been attached. DTMO also reports that more than 70% of improper DTS payments are due to inadequate or invalid receipts, and states that AOs may be held pecuniarily liable for improper payments they approve. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md]

In practice, the role is therefore best understood as [[quality assurance]] for travel payments: the AO must review evidence, apply policy, and reject or return documents that do not support payment. This makes the AO a human control point inside [[Back-office military workflow examples]] and a natural fit for [[AI-assisted spec with human review]] or [[AO helper as quality assurance manager]] patterns that surface risks without taking over the approval decision. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md, 2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-research-synthesis.md, 2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md, 2026-04-24-genai-mil-concept-seed.md]

## Key responsibilities

- Determine whether travel is necessary and appropriate to the mission
- Verify claimed expenses are valid and compliant with the Joint Travel Regulations and local business rules
- Check receipts, supporting records, and documentation completeness
- Review pre-audit flags, reason codes, advisories, and justification text
- Return vouchers that are not within policy or do not support payment ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md]

## Liability and accountability

The source material ties AO liability to the certifying function: when approval results in payment, the AO is acting as a CO, and improper certifications can create personal pecuniary liability. Public guidance also frames this liability as a reason to use standardized review steps and to treat missing or invalid evidence as a reason to return the document for correction rather than approve it. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md]
