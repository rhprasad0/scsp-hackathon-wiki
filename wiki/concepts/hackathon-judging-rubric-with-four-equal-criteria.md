# Hackathon judging rubric with four equal criteria

The GenAI.mil hackathon rubric evaluates submissions across four criteria, each weighted equally at 25%: Novelty of Approach, Technical Difficulty, Potential National Impact, and Problem-Solution Fit. The rubric is designed to balance originality, technical ambition, broader reach, and how well a team understands and addresses a real user need. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-challenge-prompt-and-rubric.md]

**Novelty of Approach (25%)** asks whether the solution brings a fresh perspective, challenges conventional thinking, introduces a new method, or tackles the problem from an unexpected angle. **Technical Difficulty (25%)** measures how technically ambitious the build is and whether the team pushed beyond off-the-shelf solutions to create something genuinely challenging. **Potential National Impact (25%)** considers whether the solution could scale and meaningfully affect communities, systems, or people across the country, with emphasis on widespread problems and credible broad reach. **Problem-Solution Fit (25%)** assesses how well the team understands the user they are building for and how directly the product addresses that need. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-challenge-prompt-and-rubric.md]

The rubric appears alongside a challenge prompt that favors narrow, operationally legible back-office workflows, such as regulation navigation, form auto-filling, TDY planning, and contract intelligence. In that context, a strong entry is expected to be both useful and technically interesting: one clear user, one end-to-end workflow, and a build that shows depth without losing focus.
