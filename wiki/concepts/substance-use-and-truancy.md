# Substance use and truancy

Substance use and truancy are described together as part of an early pattern of adolescent misbehavior and peer bonding. In the source account, alcohol and cannabis appear alongside skipping school, with these behaviors escalating in tandem as part of a developing rebellious identity. ^[2026-04-23-published-memoir-childhood-rebellion-weapons.md]

The same source frames these behaviors within a broader context of defiance against a tightly controlled Orthodox Jewish upbringing, where family authority, school discipline, and religious expectations were not effective at changing behavior. A serious school disciplinary event eventually led to expulsion, and the family later responded with an external intervention by sending the speaker to work with an uncle in Los Angeles. ^[2026-04-23-published-memoir-childhood-rebellion-weapons.md]

Related concepts include [[truancy]], [[substance use]], [[adolescent rebellion]], [[school discipline]], [[peer influence]], and [[expulsion]]. ^[2026-04-23-published-memoir-childhood-rebellion-weapons.md]
