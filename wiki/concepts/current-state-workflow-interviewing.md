# Current-State Workflow Interviewing

Current-State Workflow Interviewing is a discovery approach focused on understanding the real workflow as it exists today before writing a spec. The goal is to find enough high-signal people to validate or kill an initial wedge, using short, lightweight interviews that emphasize recent concrete workflows rather than abstract opinions about AI. In this approach, round 1 maps current-state reality and round 2 closes only the gaps that can change the spec. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

The interview workflow starts by asking organizers, track mentors, and nearby teams for people with relevant DoD, government, logistics, admin, procurement, planning, or other workflow experience. A suggested opener is to ask for 10 minutes to understand real admin, logistics, or document workflows before writing a spec. The interview should capture role or perspective, workflow trigger, step-by-step flow, artifacts, bottlenecks, trust boundaries, and open questions. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

A core practice is to focus on a specific recent case and to use prompts such as “Walk me through the last time this happened,” “What did you open first?,” “What had to be approved?,” and “What would make an AI-assisted output untrustworthy?” These questions help keep the conversation grounded in current-state reality and preserve distinctions between what AI can assist, what humans must judge, and what official systems must own. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

Current-State Workflow Interviewing also includes immediate note handling. Raw notes or speech-to-text should be promoted into curated notes right after the conversation, and the wiki should be queried after a few notes to identify repeated bottlenecks and candidate wedges. The process is meant to stop interviewing and pivot to writing when new conversations sharpen the same story rather than changing it. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

Trust and sensitivity are part of the interview method. Names should not be recorded or retained unless explicitly necessary and approved, sensitive operational details should be redacted before ingest, and interviewers should ask what information is sensitive, restricted, or awkward to share. The resulting notes should preserve the distinction between raw observations and sanitized, curated material. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

The method is designed to reduce common discovery failures such as relying on abstract opinions, drifting into solution brainstorming too early, or collecting too many weak opinions and too few concrete workflow traces. A practical stopping rule is to move toward spec writing once the same pain points and trust boundaries are repeatedly confirmed, with the source material suggesting a minimum of three independent mentions of the same pain point plus one clear trust boundary. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]
