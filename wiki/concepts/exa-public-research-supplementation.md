# Exa Public Research Supplementation

Exa Public Research Supplementation is the practice of pairing each curated note or promoted field note with targeted public search and page-fetch context from Exa. In the source material, this is presented as a way to increase evidentiary depth without changing a compiled markdown wiki workflow or adding a heavier memory or graph system. ^[2026-04-21-hackathon-knowledge-workflow-decision-public-research.md]

The rationale is that Exa’s public search and fetch capabilities are designed for AI assistants and are optimized for finding fresh web context and clean page content rather than relying on stale model memory. This makes Exa a fit for supplementing notes with public references, policy documents, product documentation, or prior reporting. ^[2026-04-21-hackathon-knowledge-workflow-decision-public-research.md]

Within the broader workflow, Exa supplementation complements the [[LLM Wiki pattern]] and [[Hash-Based Incremental Ingestion]] approach by preserving a file-first, inspectable, markdown-native process. The source material recommends keeping the underlying compiler pipeline focused on curated, redacted, high-signal notes, while using focused public supplements to support those notes. ^[2026-04-21-hackathon-knowledge-workflow-decision-public-research.md]

The source also notes a boundary condition: if the research prompt is too broad, public supplementation can introduce low-value background material instead of evidence that sharpens the question. For that reason, Exa-backed supplementation is most useful when it remains targeted and specific. ^[2026-04-21-hackathon-knowledge-workflow-decision-public-research.md]
