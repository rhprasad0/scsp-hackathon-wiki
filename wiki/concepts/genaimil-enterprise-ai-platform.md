# GenAI.mil enterprise AI platform

GenAI.mil is an enterprise generative AI platform launched on December 9, 2025 by the Department of War. It is intended for roughly 3 million military, civilian, and contractor personnel and runs through an authorized government-cloud architecture at Impact Level 5 for unclassified and CUI data. The platform uses a multi-model gateway and model isolation. Provider-specific no-training claims should be cited separately; the strongest such statement in the source note is specific to OpenAI's GenAI.mil deployment. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

At launch, GenAI.mil was described as supporting drafting and refining documents, deep research, content formatting, imagery and video analysis, and code review. Public reporting also frames it as a productivity multiplier for routine work, with leadership describing it as something that should be part of users’ daily battle rhythm. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

The platform is operated through the AI Rapid Capabilities Cell within the Department of War Office of the Under Secretary of War for Research and Engineering, with connection to the Chief Digital and Artificial Intelligence Office. Public reporting says adoption reached about 550,000 unique users in the first week, about 1 million unique users by February 2026, and about 100,000 user-built agents by April 23, 2026. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

Available models at the time of the source note included primary-sourced Gemini for Government at launch and OpenAI ChatGPT announced later. Claims about xAI Grok or a future Anthropic Claude option are based on secondary reporting or inference and should be verified before use in a public slide or spec. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

GenAI.mil sits within a broader Department of War enterprise-AI evolution that followed earlier service and task-force efforts, including Task Force Lima, NIPRGPT, and CamoGPT. Those predecessors surfaced demand and possible requirements such as multi-cloud portability, model agnosticism, and built-in safety guardrails, but internal consolidation claims should not be overstated without primary sourcing. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

For design and hackathon work, GenAI.mil defines a baseline problem space: useful applications should go beyond a simple chat interface and instead add retrieval grounding, structured output, evaluation, or an offline or on-device deployment posture.
