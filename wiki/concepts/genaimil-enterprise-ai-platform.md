# GenAI.mil enterprise AI platform

GenAI.mil is a Department of War enterprise generative AI platform launched on December 9, 2025. It is intended to provide commercial frontier-model AI to roughly 3 million uniformed military, civilian, and contractor personnel through an authorized government-cloud architecture at Impact Level 5 for unclassified and CUI data. The platform uses a multi-model gateway, isolates models, and does not allow model providers to retrain on GenAI.mil prompts or outputs. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

At launch, GenAI.mil was described as supporting drafting and refining documents, deep research, content formatting, imagery and video analysis, and code review. Public reporting also presents it as a productivity multiplier for routine work, with leadership framing it as something that should be part of users’ daily battle rhythm. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

The platform is operated through the AI Rapid Capabilities Cell within the Department of War Office of the Under Secretary of War for Research and Engineering, and is also connected to the Chief Digital and Artificial Intelligence Office. Public reporting says early adoption reached about 550,000 unique users in the first week, about 1 million unique users by February 2026, and about 100,000 user-built agents by April 23, 2026. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

Available models at the time of the source note included a Gemini for Government version at launch, OpenAI ChatGPT announced later, and xAI Grok added in late December 2025. The public context also notes that a fourth model, likely Anthropic Claude under the CDAO’s model contracts, was potentially coming. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

GenAI.mil sits within a broader Department of War enterprise-AI consolidation that followed earlier service and task-force efforts, including Task Force Lima, NIPRGPT, and CamoGPT. The public-source context treats those predecessors as early experiments that surfaced requirements such as multi-cloud portability, model agnosticism, and built-in safety guardrails, with GenAI.mil now serving as the consolidated enterprise platform. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

For hackathon and solution-design purposes, GenAI.mil defines the baseline problem space: applications should go beyond “open ChatGPT and paste in an FM” by adding a retrieval-grounded workflow, structured output, evaluation, or an offline or on-device deployment posture.
