# Curated notes promotion pipeline

The curated notes promotion pipeline is the workflow for turning raw observations into structured, redacted curated notes that can be safely ingested into a compiled wiki for later querying and synthesis. In the described operating context, it serves as the bridge between private capture surfaces and reusable working memory, helping preserve trust boundaries while making repeated pain points, workflows, artifacts, and candidate wedges easier to find. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md, 2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

The pipeline begins with private inputs such as screenshots, voice notes, raw notes, and interview transcripts or STT. These inputs are then promoted into short curated notes immediately after the conversation or capture event, with sensitive details redacted before ingest. The resulting notes are treated as durable working memory rather than a public source of truth. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md, 2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

A typical promotion workflow is to capture role or perspective, workflow trigger, step-by-step flow, artifacts, bottlenecks, trust boundaries, and open questions; then normalize that material into a concise note that can be queried alongside other curated notes. After a few notes exist, the wiki can be queried to identify repeated bottlenecks and candidate wedges, which supports a narrow reusable spec rather than broad prototype sprawl. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md, 2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]

This pipeline is intentionally aligned with a human-in-the-loop operating model. It preserves distinctions between what AI can assist, what humans must judge, and what official systems must own, and it avoids retaining names unless explicitly necessary and approved. Related concepts include [[AI-assisted spec with human review]], [[Current-State Workflow Interviewing]], [[Controlled Reference Retrieval]], and [[Scoped Prototype Wedge]]. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md, 2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]
