# Opportunistic SME discovery

Opportunistic SME discovery is an interview-efficient approach to finding and learning from subject matter experts during a short hackathon or discovery sprint. It is especially useful when access to DoD-experienced participants may be uneven, because it treats discovery as a working-memory process that can move quickly from raw observations to a narrow, reusable spec. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]

The workflow captures raw observations, screenshots, voice notes, and interview notes in Slack or local private inboxes, then promotes them into short, structured, redacted curated notes before ingesting them into a private [[llm-wiki]]. The wiki is then queried for repeated pain points, workflows, artifacts, trust boundaries, and candidate wedges, helping the team synthesize the problem without overbuilding too early. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]

This discovery style keeps raw notes and working synthesis private by default, and requires public exports to be separately sanitized and billboard-safe. It also avoids automatic outside or public research unless there is a clear evidence gap worth filling, and it expects any AI-generated spec to preserve human review and avoid inventing operational facts. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]

Opportunistic SME discovery is useful when the team wants to identify repeated pain points across independent conversations, determine which workflow has a concrete artifact and clear bottleneck, and decide when new interviews stop changing the story and start only sharpening it. It supports [[Scoped Prototype Wedge]], [[Human-in-the-Loop Review and Approval]], and [[Controlled Reference Retrieval]] by helping narrow the problem before drafting solutions. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]
