# Opportunistic SME Discovery

Opportunistic SME discovery is an interview-efficient approach to finding and learning from subject matter experts during a short hackathon or discovery sprint, especially when access to DoD-experienced participants may be uneven. It treats SME input as early working memory rather than validated truth, and uses a private wiki and curated notes to turn scattered observations into a narrow, reusable spec. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]

The workflow emphasizes capturing raw observations, screenshots, voice notes, and interview notes in Slack or local private inboxes; promoting them into short, structured, redacted curated notes; ingesting those notes into a private llm-wiki; and then querying the wiki for repeated pain points, workflows, artifacts, trust boundaries, and candidate wedges. This approach is intended to support synthesis without overbuilding before the problem is sufficiently scoped. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]

Because the operating context is private and working-memory oriented, raw notes and working synthesis remain private by default, and public exports must be separately sanitized and billboard-safe. The process also avoids automatic outside/public research unless there is a clear evidence gap worth filling, and any AI-generated spec is expected to preserve human review and avoid inventing operational facts. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]

This discovery style is useful when the team wants to identify repeated pain points across independent conversations, determine which workflow has a concrete artifact and clear bottleneck, and decide when new interviews stop changing the story and start only sharpening it. In that sense, opportunistic SME discovery supports [[Scoped Prototype Wedge]], [[Human-in-the-Loop Review and Approval]], and [[Controlled Reference Retrieval]] by helping narrow the problem before drafting solutions. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]
