# Autonomy in Weapon Systems Directive

The **Autonomy in Weapon Systems Directive** is a DoD directive focused on autonomous and semi-autonomous weapon systems. In the source material, it is described as applying specifically to weapon systems rather than ordinary enterprise adjudication tools, and as setting guidelines to minimize failures that could lead to unintended engagements. ^[2026-04-30-dod-ai-policy-bonfire.md]

The directive requires systems to allow commanders and operators to exercise appropriate levels of human judgment over the use of force. It also calls for realistic verification, validation, and test and evaluation, along with human-machine interfaces, transparent and auditable technologies and data sources, clear activation and deactivation behavior, and termination or operator input if constraints cannot be met. ^[2026-04-30-dod-ai-policy-bonfire.md]

In the source material, the directive is used as a high-signal reference point for autonomy, human judgment, constraints, testing, and governability. The note cautions against treating it as directly governing synthetic voucher-like adjudication systems, while drawing a broader lesson: autonomy becomes dangerous when a system can continue beyond intended constraints without human judgment or a stop path before consequential finalization. Related concepts include [[human judgment]], [[governability]], [[verification and validation]], [[test and evaluation]], and [[human-machine interface]]. ^[2026-04-30-dod-ai-policy-bonfire.md]
