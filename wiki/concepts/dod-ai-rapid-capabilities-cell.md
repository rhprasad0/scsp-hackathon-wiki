# DoD AI Rapid Capabilities Cell

The DoD AI Rapid Capabilities Cell (AI RCC) is the successor to Task Force Lima. It was established in December 2024 and is described as being run jointly by the CDAO and the Defense Innovation Unit (DIU). The cell took over Task Force Lima’s 15-use-case map and has focused on accelerating generative AI work for the Department of Defense/War. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

The AI RCC’s enterprise-management priorities explicitly include financial systems, human resources, enterprise logistics and supply chain, healthcare information management, legal analysis and compliance, procurement processes, and software development/cybersecurity. The source material places the GenAI.mil hackathon track squarely in this AI RCC-aligned space. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

In the public-source material, the AI RCC is also tied to the launch of GenAI.mil, a Department of War enterprise generative AI platform. That platform was launched on December 9, 2025, hosted in authorized government cloud at Impact Level 5, and intended to make commercial frontier-model AI available on DoD desktops for unclassified and CUI work. The AI RCC is identified as the operating organization behind that broader effort, with the platform also connected to the CDAO. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

The AI RCC’s predecessor, Task Force Lima, was a CDAO standing task force chartered in August 2023 and sunset on December 11, 2024. It catalogued roughly 180–230+ candidate use cases and grouped them into 15 areas across two categories: warfighting and enterprise management. The AI RCC inherited that map and continued the consolidation toward an enterprise platform. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]
