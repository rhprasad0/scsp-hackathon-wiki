# Responsible AI Implementation Memo

The **Responsible AI Implementation Memo** refers to the 2021 Deputy Secretary of Defense memorandum, *Implementing Responsible Artificial Intelligence in the Department of Defense*. The memo sets out a holistic, integrated, and disciplined approach to responsible AI across DoD AI capabilities, including autonomous systems, warfighting applications, and business or enterprise applications. It emphasizes trust with warfighters, end users, and the American public, and it calls for governance, oversight, test and evaluation, verification and validation, requirements validation, acquisition lifecycle care, and workforce understanding. ^[2026-04-30-dod-ai-policy-bonfire.md]

In the source material, the memo is used as a policy anchor for evaluating whether an AI system preserves a real [[Human Authority Boundary]] or merely produces the artifacts of compliance. The key concern is not simply whether a system makes the right decision, but whether there is an actual responsible party, independent oversight, a lifecycle boundary, and a requirements constraint that still govern the system’s behavior. ^[2026-04-30-dod-ai-policy-bonfire.md]

The memo is also relevant to synthetic evaluation of closed-loop adjudication systems, where a model can ingest packets, make decisions, generate rationales, produce audit trails, and then self-certify governance status. In that framing, a system may appear compliant while collapsing governance into generated artifacts, making the memo useful as a reference point for distinguishing real oversight from [[decorative human review]] or [[policy laundering]]. ^[2026-04-30-dod-ai-policy-bonfire.md]

A practical implication drawn from the source material is that “responsible AI” should not be reduced to self-assessment or paperwork. The memo’s implementation themes are treated as requirements for real oversight: human authority, testing and assurance, and lifecycle governance must exist independently of the system’s own claims. Related concepts include [[human-in-the-loop]], [[audit logs]], [[closed-loop adjudication pipeline]], and [[workflow automation]]. ^[2026-04-30-dod-ai-policy-bonfire.md]
