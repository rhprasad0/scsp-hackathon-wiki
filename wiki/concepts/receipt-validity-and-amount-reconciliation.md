# Receipt validity and amount reconciliation

Receipt validity and amount reconciliation are two linked review steps in DTS voucher oversight. Receipt validity asks whether the supporting receipt meets the required standards for documentation, while amount reconciliation asks whether the claimed expense matches the attached receipt or other supporting evidence. In AO review workflows, these checks are part of confirming that a voucher is valid, compliant, and supported before approval. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md]

Public DTMO guidance and service-level checklists treat receipts as a core control point. AOs are expected to review receipts along with trip dates and locations, travel mode, pre-audit flags, reason codes, advisories, document validity, and other supporting records. The Navy’s mandatory checklist is especially explicit about verifying lodging and airline receipts, rental car final receipts, and duplicate expenses. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md]

Receipt problems are a major source of voucher returns and improper payments. Public guidance and audit findings repeatedly identify missing, invalid, or inadequate receipts, and DTMO reports that more than 70% of improper payments are due to inadequate or invalid receipts. Common issues include lodging, rental car, and airfare documentation that does not satisfy validity requirements, as well as receipts that are missing entirely. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md, 2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-research-synthesis.md]

Amount reconciliation addresses a separate but related failure mode: the claimed amount may not match the receipt, even when the receipt is present. The public record identifies “claimed amounts that do not match attached documentation” and “expenses that do not match receipts” as recurring return reasons. This makes manual comparison between the voucher entry and the supporting evidence an important part of AO review. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md, 2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-research-synthesis.md]

These checks are especially important in back-office military workflows because the AO is expected to act as a quality reviewer rather than a rubber stamp. Tools that support this work should surface evidence, policy references, and likely problem areas, while leaving approval and signoff to the human reviewer. Related concepts include [[AO checklist-driven voucher review]], [[AI-assisted spec with human review]], and [[Controlled Reference Retrieval]]. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md, 2026-04-24-genai-mil-concept-seed.md]

## Common receipt and amount issues

- Missing, invalid, or inadequate receipts
- Claimed amounts that do not match receipts
- Duplicate expenses
- Receipts that lack the details needed for review
- Supporting records that do not match the voucher entry ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md]

## Why this matters

Receipt validity and amount reconciliation reduce avoidable returns, help standardize review, and support the AO’s responsibility to verify that payment is justified by the documentation. They are part of the broader control surface used to catch recurring failure modes before approval. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md]
