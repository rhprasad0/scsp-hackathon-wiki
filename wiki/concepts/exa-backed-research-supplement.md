# Exa-backed Research Supplement

Exa-backed Research Supplement is the public-research step in the workflow where each promoted curated note is paired with a companion note created from Exa-backed web search. The supplement adds relevant public context before the note pair is ingested into `llmwiki`, helping the wiki compound field notes with external evidence in a markdown-native, file-first process. ^[2026-04-21-hackathon-knowledge-workflow-decision.md]

In the described workflow, the supplement comes after a Slack thread has been promoted into a short, structured, redacted note in `curated/`. The companion research note is written in `research/`, typically using 3–5 relevant public sources, and then both files are compiled together into the wiki. ^[2026-04-21-hackathon-knowledge-workflow-decision.md]

The source material presents Exa as a good fit for this role because its search and fetch capabilities are intended to surface fresh web context and clean page content. Suggested public-source types include policy documents, product documentation, and prior reporting. The supplement is meant to sharpen the note, not to add broad background noise. ^[2026-04-21-hackathon-knowledge-workflow-decision.md, 2026-04-21-hackathon-knowledge-workflow-decision-public-research.md]

Exa-backed research supplementation is used to increase evidentiary depth without changing the underlying compiled markdown wiki architecture or introducing a heavier graph-memory system. It fits best in a small, high-signal corpus where incremental compilation and query-and-save synthesis can compound useful knowledge over time. ^[2026-04-21-hackathon-knowledge-workflow-decision-public-research.md]

A key constraint is focus: if the research prompt is too broad, the supplement can degrade into generic filler rather than evidence that sharpens the question. For that reason, the workflow treats Exa-backed supplements as targeted, specific additions that support the narrowest credible wedge. ^[2026-04-21-hackathon-knowledge-workflow-decision.md, 2026-04-21-hackathon-knowledge-workflow-decision-public-research.md]
