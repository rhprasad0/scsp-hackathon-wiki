# Hackathon rubric balanced across four equal criteria

The GenAI.mil challenge uses a simple, evenly weighted judging rubric: four criteria at 25% each. Teams are evaluated on Novelty of Approach, Technical Difficulty, Potential National Impact, and Problem-Solution Fit, with no single criterion dominating the score. The rubric is designed to reward projects that are both technically interesting and grounded in a real user need. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-challenge-prompt-and-rubric.md]

Novelty of Approach asks whether the solution brings a fresh perspective, challenges conventional thinking, introduces a new method, or tackles the problem from an unexpected angle. Technical Difficulty asks how technically ambitious the build is and whether the team went beyond off-the-shelf solutions to create something genuinely challenging. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-challenge-prompt-and-rubric.md]

Potential National Impact focuses on whether the solution could scale and meaningfully affect communities, systems, or people across the country, with priority given to widespread problems and credible broad reach. Problem-Solution Fit asks whether the team truly understands who they are building for, how well they identified a real user need, and how directly the product addresses that problem. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-challenge-prompt-and-rubric.md]

Together, these four equal criteria encourage a balanced submission: one that is novel, technically ambitious, broadly relevant, and tightly matched to a specific user problem. This balance aligns with the broader challenge emphasis on a clear user persona, end-to-end usefulness, and a bounded workflow wedge rather than an unfocused prototype.
