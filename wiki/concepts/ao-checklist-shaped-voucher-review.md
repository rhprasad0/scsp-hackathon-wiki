# AO checklist-shaped voucher review

AO checklist-shaped voucher review is the practice of reviewing a DTS voucher through a standardized checklist rather than relying on memory or ad hoc judgment. Public DTMO guidance says AOs should use a standardized process or checklist to reduce mistakes and oversights, and service-level guidance shows this approach becoming mandatory in some commands, especially after recurring audit findings tied to missing documentation, invalid receipts, duplicate expenses, and weak justification text. The review is meant to confirm that expenses are valid, compliant, and supported before approval. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md]

In this workflow, the Approving Official is not just a rubber stamp. The AO is expected to check trip dates and locations, travel mode, receipts, pre-audit flags, reason codes, advisories, document validity, and other supporting records before approving or returning the voucher. The Navy’s mandatory checklist is especially explicit, requiring screen-by-screen review of trip, expenses, per diem, accounting, financial summary, pre-audit, and submission screens. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md]

The checklist focus reflects recurring failure patterns in the public record. Common return reasons include missing or invalid receipts, claimed amounts that do not match attached documentation, duplicate expenses, vague or absent justification text, missing signature or DD Form 1351-2 issues, and date or location mismatches between the voucher and the underlying orders. DTMO also reports that more than 70% of improper payments are due to inadequate or invalid receipts. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md]

Service guidance uses the checklist to standardize control points and protect the AO from error and liability. For example, the Navy checklist calls for verification of lodging and airline receipts, rental car final receipts, duplicate expenses, proper reason codes, and meaningful justification rather than placeholder text. Quantico and New York Army National Guard guidance likewise warns that one-word or punctuation-only explanations are not sufficient for pre-audit flags. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md]

This review pattern is especially relevant for back-office military workflow automation and for [[AI-assisted spec with human review]]. Any supporting tool should help the AO as a quality reviewer by surfacing evidence, policy references, and likely problem areas, while leaving approval and signoff to the human reviewer. ## Checklist focus areas

- Trip dates and locations
- Travel mode
- Expenses and amount accuracy
- Supporting records and receipts
- Pre-audit flags, reason codes, and advisories
- Justification quality
- Document validity
- Duplicate expenses
- DD Form 1351-2 / signature requirements
- Mission and policy compliance ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md]

## Common return triggers

- Missing, invalid, or inadequate receipts
- Claimed amounts that do not match receipts
- Duplicate expenses
- Blank, one-character, or otherwise non-informative justification text
- Missing or incomplete DD Form 1351-2 / wet signature path
- Date or location mismatches between the voucher and the orders
- Incorrect per diem or lodging-tax handling
- Missing oral-order details when approval occurs after trip start ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md]

## Why the checklist matters

The checklist functions as a control surface for a role that carries pecuniary liability and is often performed as an additional duty. It reduces reliance on memory, makes the review process repeatable, and helps the AO identify the small number of recurring failure modes that drive most returns. In practice, the AO is expected to verify evidence and policy fit, then return the voucher if the documentation does not support payment. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md]
