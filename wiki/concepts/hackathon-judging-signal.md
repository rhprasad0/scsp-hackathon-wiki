# Hackathon judging signal

Hackathon judging signal is organizer guidance about what kinds of projects are likely to read well in a hackathon setting. In the source material, the signal came from an organizer relayed by Ryan during the SCSP hackathon and was explicitly described as judging/event-shape guidance rather than SME workflow evidence. The guidance emphasized that projects should be creative, interesting, and technically challenging. ^[2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md]

This signal changes how a project should be framed for judges. The recommended shape is to keep the workflow/spec grounding layer, but add a technical mechanism that is visible quickly in the demo, so the submission is not only a sober workflow artifact. The source material characterizes the best target as a “boring workflow, spicy mechanism”: a bounded, reviewable capability with a memorable technical wedge and demo hook. ^[2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md]

The guidance also sets an important constraint: creativity should not replace human review or trust boundaries. The final concept should make AI uncertainty, citations, missing information, and human approval visible, and public-facing repo/spec material should remain sanitized and avoid sensitive operational details. ^[2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md]

In practice, the source material suggests pairing operational credibility with hackathon-interesting mechanics. Examples of visible technical hooks include retrieval and citations, policy compliance checks, adversarial self-review, an eval harness, multi-agent critique, or provenance and audit trail. The overall goal is to preserve the grounded workflow while making the technical challenge understandable to judges at a glance. ^[2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md]
