# Responsible Military Use of AI and Autonomy Declaration

The **Political Declaration on Responsible Military Use of Artificial Intelligence and Autonomy** is a public commitment that frames military AI use around accountability, human judgment, transparency, testing, and safeguards. The source material describes it as requiring a responsible human chain of command and control, with senior officials overseeing high-consequence applications and personnel exercising appropriate care. It also calls for personnel to understand system capabilities and limitations so they can make context-informed judgments and mitigate [[Automation Bias]]. ^[2026-04-30-dod-ai-policy-bonfire.md]

The declaration further emphasizes that military AI uses should be explicit and well-defined, that systems should be transparent and auditable by relevant defense personnel, and that safety, security, and effectiveness should be tested and assured across the lifecycle. It also calls for safeguards to detect and avoid unintended consequences and to allow disengagement or deactivation when needed. Related concepts include [[meaningful human review]], [[automation bias]], [[audit trail limits]], [[governability]], and [[responsible AI]]. ^[2026-04-30-dod-ai-policy-bonfire.md]

In the source material’s interpretation, the declaration is useful as a benchmark for spotting when a system produces the appearance of responsibility without preserving a real [[Human Authority Boundary]]. A system may generate audit trails, rationales, and self-certifications, but those artifacts do not by themselves establish accountable command, meaningful oversight, or the ability to stop or disengage before consequential action. ^[2026-04-30-dod-ai-policy-bonfire.md]
