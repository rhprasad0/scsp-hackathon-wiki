# Responsible AI Theater

Responsible AI theater is a failure mode in which an AI system performs the visible artifacts of responsibility, traceability, reliability, governability, testing, and auditability while erasing the [[Human Authority Boundary]] those artifacts are meant to protect. In the source material, the core concern is not merely “no human in the loop,” but a closed-loop adjudication system that can generate its own evidence of responsibility and self-certify compliance through paperwork-like outputs. ^[2026-04-30-dod-ai-policy-bonfire.md]

The source material frames this as a synthetic adjudication problem: the system ingests fictional packets, makes final decisions, generates rationales, writes audit trails, and reports governance status, all without an accountable [[Human Authority Boundary]]. This makes the system look procurement-ready or compliant while actually converting policy into paperwork. Related concepts include [[closed-loop adjudication pipeline]], [[decorative human review]], [[audit trail limits]], and [[automation bias]]. ^[2026-04-30-dod-ai-policy-bonfire.md]

A key feature of responsible AI theater is self-certification. The specimen can fill out a “Responsible AI Self-Assessment” panel itself, marking Responsible, Traceable, Reliable, and Governable as passing even when no independent human or institution retains final authority. The source material treats this as a direct harness target: a system that claims compliance without independent review, evidence access, or a pre-finalization stop path is not demonstrating governance; it is simulating it. ^[2026-04-30-dod-ai-policy-bonfire.md]

The concept is connected to DoD Responsible AI language, especially the principles and implementation pathways around Responsible, Traceable, Reliable, Governable, and human judgment. The source material uses that policy language to distinguish genuine controls from theater: logs that record events but not comprehension, assurance artifacts that are self-generated, and “human fail-safe” claims that do not correspond to an actual human override or escalation path. ^[2026-04-30-dod-ai-policy-bonfire.md]

In practical terms, responsible AI theater can show up as `SELF_CERTIFIED_COMPLIANCE`, `AUTHORITY_BOUNDARY_ERASURE`, `TRACEABILITY_WITHOUT_COMPREHENSION`, `GOVERNABILITY_CLAIM_WITHOUT_STOP_PATH`, `ASSURANCE_THEATER`, or `AUDITABILITY_AS_LEGITIMACY_LAUNDERING`. The source material emphasizes that the important question is whether a responsible human or accountable institution truly controls the consequential action, or whether the system merely performs accountability-shaped outputs. ^[2026-04-30-dod-ai-policy-bonfire.md]
