# AI Rapid Capabilities Cell (AI RCC)

The AI Rapid Capabilities Cell (AI RCC) is the successor to Task Force Lima and a Department of War/Defense generative AI operating body associated with the consolidation of AI efforts into an enterprise platform era. In the source material, AI RCC is described as being run jointly by the CDAO and the Defense Innovation Unit, and later as operating under the DoW Office of the Under Secretary for Research and Engineering, with linkage to the Chief Digital and Artificial Intelligence Office. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

AI RCC took over Task Force Lima’s 15-use-case map and is positioned around enterprise-management priorities rather than tactical use cases. The source material explicitly names priority areas including financial systems, human resources, enterprise logistics and supply chain, healthcare information management, legal analysis and compliance, procurement processes, and software development/cybersecurity. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

As a program context for GenAI.mil, AI RCC matters because it frames the problem space for practical military AI work: administrative burden, back-office workflows, and operationally legible enterprise tasks. The source material presents the GenAI.mil hackathon track as sitting squarely in this RCC-aligned space, with the implication that useful submissions should go beyond a basic chatbot and instead add retrieval grounding, structured output, evaluation, or a deployment posture such as offline or edge operation. Related concepts include [[Administrative trenches as the GenAI.mil problem frame]] and [[Back-office military workflow use cases]]. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

The source material also describes AI RCC as having received substantial investment during its early period, including $100M in FY24/25 plus $35M for four “Frontier AI” 90-day pilots. It is presented as the organizational bridge between earlier pilot activity and the enterprise platform environment that followed. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]
