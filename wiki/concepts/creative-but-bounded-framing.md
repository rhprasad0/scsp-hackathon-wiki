# Creative but bounded framing

Creative but bounded framing is a presentation strategy for hackathon projects in which the project is made memorable, interesting, and technically challenging, while still staying narrow enough to remain credible and easy to explain. The organizing signal recommends a shape best summarized as “boring workflow, spicy mechanism”: keep a grounded workflow story, but add a visible technical wedge so the project does not read as a generic assistant or an unbounded prototype. ^[2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md]

In this framing, creativity does not mean removing human review or widening scope. The concept should preserve trust boundaries, keep AI uncertainty, citations, missing information, and human approval visible, and ensure any public-facing repo or spec remains sanitized. This makes the creative element legible without making the system look reckless. ^[2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md]

A strong implementation target is a single, reviewable workflow with a memorable technical hook, such as constrained AI-assisted artifact generation paired with retrieval, structured drafting, self-critique, or evaluation. The source material emphasizes that the technically challenging part should be visible in the demo and story, not buried in implementation details. ^[2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md]

This framing is designed to balance two failure modes: projects that are safe but dull, and projects that are flashy but untethered from real workflow evidence. It favors a narrow capability, a clear risk frame, and outputs that remain compatible with human approval and accountable decision-making. ^[2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md]
