# Amount reconciliation against receipts and exchange-rate math

Amount reconciliation against receipts and exchange-rate math is the review step that checks whether claimed expense amounts line up with the supporting receipt, local paperwork, card transaction, or currency conversion math. In practitioner observations from DTS review, this is treated as part of a broader audit-like reconstruction problem: each expense must be entered with the amount paid, where it was paid, and a spending category, and the reviewer looks for “strange numbers” that do not match expected patterns, hand-calculated currency conversions, or local receipts that need to add up correctly. ^[2026-04-25-dts-practitioner-response-batch-1.md]

This check is especially important when a packet includes cash, ATM withdrawals, exchange rates, or local receipts from OCONUS or third-country vendors. The source material notes that these situations often produce weaker documentation and make reconstruction harder, so the reviewer may need to compare the claimed amount against the receipt and the conversion math rather than relying on a clean card record. Related concepts include [[AO checklist-driven voucher review]] and [[AI-assisted spec with human review]]. ^[2026-04-25-dts-practitioner-response-batch-1.md]

A mismatch does not automatically prove the expense is invalid, but it is a strong review signal. The practitioner notes identify high complexity, missing dates, overlapping or inconsistent details, multiple charges where one would normally be expected, and unusual hotel or currency-related amounts as indicators that a packet may need closer human scrutiny. The review goal is to determine whether the packet explains itself well enough for an accountable reviewer. ^[2026-04-25-dts-practitioner-response-batch-1.md]

For automation and triage, the most useful output is not approval or denial but a clear flag that the amount needs human reconciliation. Suggested checks include whether each expense has a legible receipt or acceptable substitute, whether claimed amounts match receipts or exchange-rate math, whether there are unexplained deltas, and whether ATM or cash transactions need supporting evidence and math review. ^[2026-04-25-dts-practitioner-response-batch-1.md]
