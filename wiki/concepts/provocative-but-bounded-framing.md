# Provocative but bounded framing

Provocative but bounded framing is a way of presenting a project so it feels memorable, sharp, and a little edgy without becoming so broad that the evaluator has to reconstruct the story. The source material describes strong hackathon projects as narrower, sharper, and more demoable than broad repositories, and notes that the best concepts are “provocative but bounded”: enough edge to stand out, but not so much sprawl that the main idea gets lost. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md]

This framing favors a concrete threat, workflow, or infrastructure class over a generic platform pitch. In the source material, winning patterns are described as having a single protected asset or diagnostic wedge, with the prototype expressible in one sentence and easy to understand quickly. The goal is to be specific enough to communicate operational relevance and human oversight, while avoiding a toolbox-style bundle of dashboards, agents, maps, chatbots, and uploads. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md]

A provocative-but-bounded concept is easier to judge because its trust boundaries are visible. The source material highlights artifacts that make clear what the AI may do, what it may not do, where human accountability remains, and how review or approval happens before anything leaves the tool. It also emphasizes missing-information flags, assumptions separated from facts, and citations to approved references as part of that legibility. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md]

The framing is closely tied to evidence-backed narrowing. Rather than expanding broadly from available AI primitives, the recommended pattern is to start with a specific workflow wedge, validate it with concrete evidence, and treat early findings as hypothesis scaffolding until repeated signals confirm the pain point and trust boundary. This keeps the concept provocative enough to be memorable while remaining bounded by an evidence base. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md, 2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]
