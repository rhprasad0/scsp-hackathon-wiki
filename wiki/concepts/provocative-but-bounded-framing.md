# Provocative-but-Bounded Framing

Provocative-but-Bounded Framing is a project-framing approach that aims to make a hackathon concept memorable and legible without letting it expand into broad prototype sprawl. The source material characterizes strong projects as narrower, sharper, and more demoable than many broader repos, and notes that the best concepts are “provocative but bounded”: enough edge to stand out, but not so much sprawl that the evaluator has to reconstruct the story. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md]

This framing favors a concrete threat, workflow, or infrastructure class over a generic platform pitch. The winning-pattern examples emphasize a single protected asset or diagnostic wedge, with the prototype expressible in one sentence and easy to understand quickly. In practice, the concept should be specific enough to show operational relevance and human oversight, while avoiding a toolbox-style bundle of dashboards, agents, maps, chatbots, and uploads. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md]

A provocative-but-bounded project is usually easier to judge because it makes its trust boundaries visible. The source material highlights artifacts that show what the AI is allowed to do, what it is forbidden to do, where human accountability remains, and how review or approval happens before anything leaves the tool. This is paired with missing-information flags, assumptions separated from facts, and citations to approved references. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md]

The concept is also tied to evidence-backed narrowing. Rather than expanding broadly from available AI primitives, the recommended pattern is to start with a specific workflow wedge, validate it with concrete evidence, and treat early findings as hypothesis scaffolding until enough repeated signals confirm the pain point and trust boundary. This keeps the framing provocative enough to be memorable while still remaining bounded by an evidence base. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md, 2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]
