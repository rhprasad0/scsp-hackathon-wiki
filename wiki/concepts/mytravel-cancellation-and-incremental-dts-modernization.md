# MyTravel cancellation and incremental DTS modernization

MyTravel was discontinued in May 2023, and the current public record describes Department of Defense travel-system change as an incremental modernization of [[Defense Travel System (DTS)|DTS]] rather than a replacement platform. The available synthesis characterizes this as a six-year improvement plan scheduled to begin in FY2027, with current updates framed as controls and enhancements inside DTS. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-research-synthesis.md]

This matters for tool design because it places any new assistant in a companion role to current DTS workflows, not as a successor system. The synthesis explicitly recommends treating the AI assistant as a public-safe aid for the existing DTS environment, with no claim of live DTS integration or on-platform deployment. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-research-synthesis.md]

In this context, the most defensible modernization wedge is pre-decision support: evidence packaging, queue prioritization, explanation assistance, and review support before an Approving Official stamps a voucher. That positioning complements post-approval compliance tools such as the Travel Policy Compliance Tool, which operate after approval rather than as a replacement for human review. Related concepts include [[AI-assisted spec with human review]] and [[AO checklist-driven voucher review]]. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-research-synthesis.md]
