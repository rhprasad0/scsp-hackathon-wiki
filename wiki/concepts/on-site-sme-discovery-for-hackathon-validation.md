# On-site [[SME Discovery for Hackathon Validation]]

On-site [[SME Discovery for Hackathon Validation]] is a short, direct interview process used during a hackathon to find enough high-signal subject-matter experts to validate or reject an initial project wedge. The discovery goal is not to write the final spec first, but to quickly test whether the proposed workflow is grounded in real current-state practice. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

The approach favors lightweight outreach to organizers, track mentors, nearby teams, and other participants who may have DoD, defense, logistics, admin, procurement, planning, or government workflow experience. Because organizers may not reliably route participants to the right experts, the plan relies on direct asks and short, specific openers rather than waiting for formal introductions. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

Interviews should center on recent concrete workflows rather than abstract opinions about AI. A recommended opener is: “I’m trying to understand real admin/logistics/document workflows before writing a spec. Could I ask you for 10 minutes about a recent workflow you’ve seen?” The interview should capture the role or perspective, the trigger for the workflow, the step-by-step flow, the artifacts involved, bottlenecks, trust boundaries, and open questions. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

The process is organized in two rounds. Round 1 maps current-state reality; Round 2 addresses only the gaps that can actually change the spec. After each conversation, raw notes or speech-to-text should be promoted immediately into a curated note, then queried in a wiki after a few interviews to look for repeated bottlenecks and candidate wedges. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

The discovery loop is meant to stop once new conversations sharpen the same story instead of changing it. At that point, the team should pivot from interviewing to writing, using the repeated pain points and trust boundaries as the evidence base for the spec. The plan suggests a minimum of about three independent mentions of the same pain point, plus one clear trust boundary, before writing. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

A few practical constraints shape the process. Teams should not record or retain names unless explicitly necessary and approved, should redact sensitive operational details before ingest, and should ask what information is sensitive, restricted, or awkward to share. The goal is to preserve the distinction between what AI can assist, what humans must judge, and what official systems must own. Related concepts include [[Current-State Workflow Interviewing]], [[AI-assisted spec with human review]], and [[Scoped Prototype Wedge]]. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]
