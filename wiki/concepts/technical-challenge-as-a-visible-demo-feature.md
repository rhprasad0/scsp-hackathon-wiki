# Technical challenge as a visible demo feature

“Technical challenge as a visible demo feature” is the idea that a hackathon submission should make its difficult engineering work easy to see in the demo, not just hidden in the implementation. The organizer guidance says projects should be creative, interesting, and technically challenging, and notes that the technical challenge must be visible in the story and demo rather than buried in the code. ^[2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md]

In the GenAI.mil context, this means a solution should not be only a sober workflow or spec artifact. It should pair a grounded, operationally credible use case with a memorable technical wedge and demo hook, so judges can quickly understand both the utility and the complexity of the system. ^[2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md]

The recommended shape is “boring workflow, spicy mechanism”: keep the workflow narrow and believable, but add a mechanism that makes the system feel technically sophisticated. Examples from the source material include retrieval and citations, policy compliance checks, adversarial self-review, an evaluation harness, multi-agent critique, and provenance or audit trails. ^[2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md]

This visible technical challenge should also preserve trust boundaries. The source guidance explicitly says creativity should not remove human review, and that AI uncertainty, citations, missing information, and human approval should remain visible. That makes the demo feel both capable and accountable. ^[2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md]

In practice, the feature works best when tied to a [[Scoped Prototype Wedge]] or other bounded workflow: one clear task, one understandable output, and one technical mechanism that can be explained quickly. For GenAI.mil-style projects, this helps avoid both a flashy toy with weak workflow evidence and a useful but boring spec that fails to stand out. Related concepts include [[Boring workflow, spicy mechanism]], [[AI-assisted spec with human review]], and [[Citations and missing-field visibility]]. ^[2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md]
