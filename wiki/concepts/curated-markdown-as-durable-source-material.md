# Curated markdown as durable source material

Curated markdown is the durable source material in the hackathon workflow: raw observations, screenshots, voice notes, and interview notes are first promoted into short, structured, redacted curated notes before ingest. Those notes are then compiled into the private wiki, where they serve as working memory for synthesis rather than as a public source of truth. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]

In this workflow, curated markdown sits between raw capture and downstream artifacts. The process starts in Slack or local private inboxes, where material is collected, then moves into curated notes that are suitable for ingestion into llm-wiki. The compiled wiki is queried for repeated pain points, workflows, artifacts, trust boundaries, and candidate wedges, and only public-safe summaries or spec fragments are exported downstream. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]

The emphasis on curation reflects the project’s trust boundaries. Raw notes and working synthesis remain private by default, public-facing exports must be separately sanitized, and any AI-generated spec should preserve human review and avoid inventing operational facts. The source material also treats the early evidence base as hypothesis scaffolding rather than validated SME signal, so curated markdown supports a narrow, evidence-backed synthesis rather than broad prototype sprawl. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]
