# Public-safe unclassified prototype posture

Public-safe unclassified prototype posture is the operating stance for a GenAI.mil prototype that uses only public, unclassified, or otherwise shareable material and is intended to remain safe for public submission and review. It excludes controlled information, PII, and classified or operational detail, and it keeps outputs publicly shareable throughout the event. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-user-workflows-and-trust-boundaries.md]

This posture depends on clear trust boundaries. The AI may draft, retrieve, cite, structure, and pre-flight content, but it may not approve, submit, classify, sign, file, or take kinetic action. Final action remains with the user or another authorized human through the official path. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-user-workflows-and-trust-boundaries.md]

A public-safe prototype should also be conservative when grounding is weak. The source material emphasizes paragraph-level citations for retrieval-grounded answers, hard refusal when the corpus does not support a claim, and visible uncertainty when a statement cannot be anchored to a source. Wrong or uncited guidance is treated as mission risk, especially for policy, pay, travel, or other administrative records. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-user-workflows-and-trust-boundaries.md]

The expected data posture is unclassified and shareable. The source material points to public Army publications, field manuals, forms, the Joint Travel Regulations, SAM.gov, USAspending.gov, eCFR Title 48, and similar public reference sets, and it states that classified data does not enter the hackathon environment. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-user-workflows-and-trust-boundaries.md]

For content handling, the posture favors sanitized drafts and reviewable outputs. The described workflow captures raw observations privately, promotes them into structured redacted notes, and exports only billboard-safe or public-safe material. Downstream artifacts should show sources, assumptions, unresolved fields, uncertainty, and review status. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md, 2026-04-24-genai-mil-concept-seed.md]

In practice, this posture supports narrow administrative and back-office workflows such as regulation Q&A, leave-form drafting, TDY planning, and contract-intel scaffolding. These are framed as public-safe, unclassified use cases with clear evidence sources and a believable user, rather than tactical or kinetic applications. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-challenge-prompt-and-rubric.md, 2026-04-25-genai-mil-user-workflows-and-trust-boundaries.md]
