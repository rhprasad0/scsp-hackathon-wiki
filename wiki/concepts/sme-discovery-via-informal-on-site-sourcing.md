# SME Discovery via Informal On-Site Sourcing

SME Discovery via Informal On-Site Sourcing is a hackathon discovery approach for finding and interviewing people with relevant DoD, defense, logistics, admin, procurement, planning, or government workflow experience while on site. The goal is not to write the final spec first, but to quickly locate enough high-signal people to validate or reject the initial wedge. Because organizers may not reliably route participants to the right subject-matter experts, the approach relies on direct, lightweight asks to nearby people, mentors, and teams. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

The interviews should focus on recent, concrete workflows rather than abstract opinions about AI. A short opener can be used to ask for ten minutes about a recent admin, logistics, or document workflow, and then the conversation should probe the workflow trigger, step-by-step flow, artifacts, bottlenecks, trust boundaries, and open questions. The plan emphasizes mapping current-state reality in a first round, then using a second round only to close gaps that could change the spec. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

A central practice is to capture each conversation as raw notes or speech-to-text, then promote it immediately into a curated note for ingest. After a few notes, the wiki can be queried for repeated bottlenecks and candidate wedges. Interviewing should stop and writing should begin once new conversations start sharpening the same story instead of changing it. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

The approach includes several trust boundaries. Names should not be recorded or retained unless explicitly necessary and approved, sensitive operational details should be redacted before ingest, and participants should be asked what information is sensitive, restricted, or awkward to share. The process also preserves the distinction between what AI can assist with, what humans must judge, and what official systems must own. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

Common failure modes include SMEs being scarce or busy, participants speaking in abstractions, conversations drifting into solution brainstorming too early, and the team collecting too many weak opinions instead of concrete workflow traces. The source material suggests using a spec-readiness signal such as red, yellow, or green, and treating repeated pain points plus at least one clear trust boundary as evidence that the discovery phase is mature enough to pivot toward spec writing. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]
