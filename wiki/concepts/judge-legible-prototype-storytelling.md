# Judge-Legible Prototype Storytelling

Judge-Legible Prototype Storytelling is the practice of presenting a prototype as a narrow, operationally clear use case that a judge can understand quickly. The source material emphasizes that strong projects are easier to evaluate when they show one concrete threat, workflow, or infrastructure class, make the prototype legible in a single sentence, and preserve an explicit trust boundary rather than hiding accountability behind broad feature sets. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md]

This storytelling style favors a specific wedge over prototype sprawl. Rather than framing a project as a general-purpose platform with dashboards, agents, maps, chat, or upload features, the prototype should be memorable as a bounded workflow with a clear purpose, trusted references, missing fields or assumptions, and a defined approval path. The material describes this as a way to keep the evaluator from having to assemble the story themselves. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md]

In the GenAI.mil-oriented examples, judge-legible storytelling pairs a serious operational frame with human oversight. The preferred shape is a low-side assistant that turns messy requests into cited, reviewable drafts while preserving human approval, making the basis of the output visible through sources, assumptions, unresolved fields, uncertainty, and review status. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md, 2026-04-24-genai-mil-concept-seed.md]

Judge-legible prototypes are also tied to evidence and workflow specificity. The source material recommends a narrow, high-evidence workflow wedge, supported by [[Controlled Reference Retrieval]] and current-state interviewing, so that the resulting concept reflects recent concrete workflows and approved references rather than abstract opinions or invented operational facts. ^[2026-04-24-genai-mil-concept-seed.md, 2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

A useful pattern is to be able to describe the prototype in one sentence that answers: who uses it, what artifact they need to produce, what trusted references constrain it, what missing fields or assumptions must be flagged, and who approves it before it leaves the tool. This kind of description makes the workflow easy for judges to parse and highlights where human judgment remains required. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md, 2026-04-24-genai-mil-concept-seed.md]
