# Corrective intervention by an uncle in Los Angeles

A **corrective intervention by an uncle in Los Angeles** refers to the family response described in the source account, in which repeated behavioral problems led to an external intervention: the speaker was sent to work with an uncle in Los Angeles. The intervention is presented as a deliberate attempt to impose discipline through labor and exposure to harsher realities after family authority, punishment, and school discipline had not reliably changed behavior. ^[2026-04-23-published-memoir-childhood-rebellion-weapons.md]

In the source narrative, this step comes after a pattern of youth rebellion that includes religious doubt, truancy, substance experimentation, and peer-group misbehavior, culminating in a serious school disciplinary event and expulsion. The uncle is described as a higher-authority corrective figure, and the Los Angeles setting is part of the attempt to redirect the speaker away from escalating defiance.
