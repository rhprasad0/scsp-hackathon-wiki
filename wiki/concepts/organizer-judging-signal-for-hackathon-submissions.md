# Organizer judging signal for hackathon submissions

Organizer judging signal refers to informal event-shape guidance about what hackathon submissions are likely to score well with judges. In the source material, the organizer signal says projects should be **creative**, **interesting**, and **technically challenging**. It also emphasizes that a submission should not be only a sober workflow or spec artifact; it needs a memorable technical wedge and a demo hook. ^[2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md]

This signal does not replace grounded user or workflow evidence. Instead, it raises the presentation bar: the project should be both operationally credible and hackathon-interesting. The guidance warns against over-correcting in either direction, since a flashy toy can lose workflow legitimacy and a purely rigorous spec can come across as boring in judging. ^[2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md]

A practical framing recommended by the organizer signal is **boring workflow, spicy mechanism**. In this pattern, the underlying task stays narrow and believable, while the AI system adds technical interest through retrieval, citations, structured drafting, self-critique, evaluation, or a trust-boundary visualization. The source material also suggests that the technical challenge should be visible in the demo and story, not buried in implementation details. ^[2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md]

The guidance explicitly says creativity should not remove human review. Final concepts should make AI uncertainty, citations, missing information, and human approval visible, and any public-facing repo or spec should remain sanitized and avoid sensitive operational details. ^[2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md]
