# Workflow artifact and bottleneck capture

Workflow artifact and bottleneck capture is a note-taking and synthesis practice for documenting a recent, concrete workflow in enough detail to understand the role or perspective of the person interviewed, what triggered the workflow, the step-by-step process, which artifacts were used or produced, and where the process slowed down or failed. The discovery plan frames this as a way to ground the wiki in current-state reality rather than abstract opinions, so repeated pain points and candidate wedges can be identified from the compiled notes. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

The capture process is tied to a broader interview workflow. Interviewers are expected to promote raw notes or speech-to-text records into curated notes immediately after the conversation, then query the wiki after a few interviews to identify repeated bottlenecks. This supports a narrow, evidence-backed spec and helps determine when additional interviews are no longer changing the story. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

Artifact capture matters because the interviews are meant to reflect real administrative, logistics, document, procurement, planning, or government work. Prompts such as “Walk me through the last time this happened,” “What did you open first?” and “What had to be approved?” are used to preserve the concrete sequence of steps and the artifacts involved, rather than drifting into general discussion or solution brainstorming. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

Bottleneck capture focuses on where the workflow becomes difficult or unreliable. The source material highlights common failure modes such as scarce or busy SMEs, abstract answers, premature solution brainstorming, and collecting too many weak opinions instead of concrete workflow traces. Recording these bottlenecks alongside the workflow helps distinguish real process constraints from speculative problems. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

Trust boundaries are part of the capture process as well. Interviewers are told not to record or retain names unless explicitly necessary and approved, to redact sensitive operational details before ingest, and to ask what information is sensitive, restricted, or awkward to share. The notes should also preserve distinctions between what AI can assist with, what humans must judge, and what official systems must own. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

A practical output of this capture process is a curated interview debrief that can be compared across multiple interviews. The source material suggests waiting for 3+ independent mentions of the same pain point plus one clear trust boundary before writing, and it uses a spec-readiness score of red, yellow, or green to indicate whether the evidence base is ready to pivot into drafting.
