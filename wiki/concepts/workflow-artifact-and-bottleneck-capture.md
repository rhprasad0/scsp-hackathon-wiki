# Workflow Artifact and Bottleneck Capture

Workflow Artifact and Bottleneck Capture is the practice of documenting a recent, concrete workflow in enough detail to understand what was done, what artifacts were used or produced, and where the process slowed down or failed. In the discovery plan, interviewers are instructed to capture the role or perspective of the person interviewed, the workflow trigger, the step-by-step flow, artifacts, bottlenecks, trust boundaries, and open questions. The intent is to ground the wiki in current-state reality rather than abstract opinions, so that repeated pain points and candidate wedges can later be identified from the compiled notes. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

The workflow is tied to a broader capture-and-synthesis process. Raw notes or speech-to-text records are promoted into curated notes immediately after the conversation, and those curated notes are then queried in the wiki after a few interviews to identify repeated bottlenecks. This supports a narrow, evidence-backed spec and helps the team decide when new conversations are no longer changing the story. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

Artifact capture matters because the source workflow is centered on real administrative, logistics, document, procurement, planning, or government tasks. Interview prompts emphasize specific recent cases, such as “Walk me through the last time this happened,” “What did you open first?” and “What had to be approved?” These prompts help preserve the concrete sequence of steps and the artifacts involved, instead of drifting into general discussion or solution brainstorming. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

Bottleneck capture focuses on where the workflow becomes difficult or unreliable. The source material highlights several common failure modes: SMEs may be scarce or busy, participants may answer in abstractions, conversations may drift into ideas before the current workflow is understood, and teams may collect too many weak opinions instead of concrete workflow traces. Capturing these bottlenecks alongside the workflow makes it possible to distinguish real process constraints from speculative problems. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

Trust boundaries are part of the capture process as well. Interviewers are told not to record or retain names unless explicitly necessary and approved, to redact sensitive operational details before ingest, and to ask what information is sensitive, restricted, or awkward to share. The notes should preserve distinctions between what AI can assist with, what humans must judge, and what official systems must own. ^[2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]

A practical output of this capture process is a curated interview debrief that can be compared across multiple interviews. The source material suggests a minimum of 3+ independent mentions of the same pain point plus one clear trust boundary before writing, and it includes a “spec-readiness score” of red, yellow, or green to help indicate whether the evidence base is ready to pivot into drafting.
