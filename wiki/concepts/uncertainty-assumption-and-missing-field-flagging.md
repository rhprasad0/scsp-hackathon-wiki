# Uncertainty, assumption, and missing-field flagging

Uncertainty, assumption, and missing-field flagging is a drafting and review practice in which an AI system makes its basis visible instead of presenting a draft as fully certain or complete. In the source material, the system may generate structured drafts, cite approved references, identify missing fields, mark assumptions and inferred content, and flag low-confidence claims so a human reviewer can evaluate the result before export or submission. ^[2026-04-24-genai-mil-concept-seed.md]

This practice is tied to preserving trust boundaries in AI-assisted workflows. The material emphasizes that the assistant should help produce a correct first draft faster, but must not invent policy, approve work, autonomously submit outputs, or bypass review. Visibility into uncertainty and unresolved inputs is part of what makes the draft reviewable rather than something a user must accept on faith. ^[2026-04-24-genai-mil-concept-seed.md]

Missing-field flagging is especially important in routine administrative and logistics workflows, where rework can come from absent information, wrong format, unclear routing, or ungrounded assumptions. The draft process therefore includes identifying required missing fields and asking for them before generating a final draft, so that the resulting artifact is narrower, more complete, and easier for a human reviewer to approve or revise. Related concepts include [[AI-assisted spec with human review]], [[Controlled Reference Retrieval]], and [[Human-in-the-Loop Review and Approval]]. ^[2026-04-24-genai-mil-concept-seed.md]

The source material also frames this as part of a broader reviewable workflow: the AI may draft, cite, structure, and flag uncertainty, while a human reviewer edits, accepts, rejects, or asks follow-up questions. Users should be able to see assumptions and unresolved fields before submission, and trust depends on making the draft’s basis visible, including sources, assumptions, uncertainty, and review status. ^[2026-04-24-genai-mil-concept-seed.md, 2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]
