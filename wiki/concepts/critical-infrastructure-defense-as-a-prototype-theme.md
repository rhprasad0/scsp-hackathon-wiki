# Critical Infrastructure Defense as a Prototype Theme

Critical Infrastructure Defense as a Prototype Theme refers to a prototype direction that is narrow, bounded, and operationally legible: it frames the work around defending a specific protected asset, rather than trying to “protect all infrastructure” or building a broad AI platform. In the source material, a winning-style example is an AI-powered cybersecurity prototype for U.S. critical infrastructure, initially focused on water treatment systems, with explicit human-in-the-loop support for detection and response. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md]

This theme fits the broader pattern that strong hackathon projects are sharper and more demoable than generic “agentic AI for government” concepts. The project should be understandable in one sentence, show a clear risk frame, and make the operational relevance visible quickly to judges or reviewers. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md]

A useful prototype wedge in this theme is to center on a concrete infrastructure class and a concrete security workflow, such as anomaly detection, LLM-assisted analysis, retrieval over trusted references, or incident response support. The source material suggests that the best projects pair prototype ingredients like anomaly detection, RAG, agents, and real-time industrial sensor analysis with a clear human oversight posture, rather than autonomous replacement of accountable people. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md]

The main design constraint is to avoid breadth masquerading as capability. Adding dashboards, maps, chatbots, uploads, and other features can obscure the core operational value if they are not tied to the specific infrastructure workflow being defended. The prototype should remain a wedge: one protected asset, one recurring problem, one reviewable output. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md]
