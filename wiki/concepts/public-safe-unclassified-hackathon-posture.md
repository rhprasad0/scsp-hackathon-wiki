# Public-safe unclassified hackathon posture

Public-safe unclassified hackathon posture is a development and demo stance for hackathon projects that stays within unclassified and CUI-safe boundaries, keeps outputs sanitized, and is suitable for public-facing review. In the GenAI.mil context, this posture fits the enterprise, back-office problem space: it avoids tactical or kinetic use cases and instead emphasizes administrative workflows, controlled references, and reviewable outputs. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md, 2026-04-25-genai-mil-challenge-prompt-and-rubric.md]

A public-safe posture assumes that the system can work from approved public DoD text or other public sources, with retrieval grounding and visible citations rather than free-form answers that imply authority. The source material favors small, narrow workflows that answer concrete questions, draft structured artifacts, and flag uncertainty or missing fields, while preserving human review before any submission or export. Related concepts include [[Controlled Reference Retrieval]], [[AI-assisted spec with human review]], and [[Citations and missing-field visibility]]. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md, 2026-04-24-genai-mil-concept-seed.md, 2026-04-25-genai-mil-user-workflows-and-trust-boundaries.md]

This posture also aligns with the “offline” interpretation of the hackathon prompt: a system that can function without persistent cloud connectivity, and that complements rather than duplicates the broader enterprise GenAI.mil platform. In practical terms, that means a bounded assistant built around a public DoD corpus, with controlled drafting, reviewable provenance, and no dependence on secret or operationally sensitive inputs. Related concepts include [[Administrative trenches as the GenAI.mil problem frame]] and [[Back-office military workflow use cases]]. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md, 2026-04-25-genai-mil-challenge-prompt-and-rubric.md]

The trust posture should remain visible throughout the workflow: show sources, assumptions, unresolved fields, uncertainty, and review status; do not invent policy; and do not approve, autonomously submit, or bypass human review. This makes the demo legible to judges while keeping the project within a public-safe, unclassified operating envelope. Related concepts include [[Human-in-the-Loop Review and Approval]] and [[draft review workspace]]. ^[2026-04-24-genai-mil-concept-seed.md, 2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]
