# Judge-legible prototype story

A judge-legible prototype story is a way of presenting a project so a judge can quickly understand the user, the workflow, the trusted references, the missing fields or assumptions, and the approval path. In the source material, strong hackathon projects are described as narrow, sharp, and demoable, with a clear problem and a prototype that can be explained in one sentence. The goal is not just to show capability, but to make the operational value and trust boundaries immediately visible. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md]

This kind of story is especially useful in the GenAI.mil context, where successful concepts are framed as bounded workflow wedges rather than broad AI assistants. A judge-legible story should identify who uses the tool, what artifact they need to produce, which approved references constrain the output, what uncertainty must be flagged, and who approves the result before it leaves the system. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md, 2026-04-25-genai-mil-challenge-prompt-and-rubric.md]

The source material suggests that the best stories are memorable because they are concrete: one real workflow, one clear output, and one technically interesting mechanism. Examples include turning messy logistics or administrative requests into cited, reviewable drafts, or building a narrow retrieval-backed tool for regulation navigation, form filling, TDY planning, or contract intelligence. The emphasis is on [[Operational relevance plus human oversight]], not on autonomous replacement of accountable people. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md, 2026-04-25-genai-mil-challenge-prompt-and-rubric.md, 2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md]

A judge-legible prototype story also makes trust boundaries explicit. The material recommends showing sources, assumptions, unresolved fields, uncertainty, and review status, and preserving human review before approval or submission. This helps the judge see what the AI is allowed to do, what it is forbidden to do, and how the user remains responsible for the final decision. Related concepts include [[AI-assisted spec with human review]], [[Boring workflow, spicy mechanism]], [[Controlled Reference Retrieval]], and [[Citations and missing-field visibility]]. ^[2026-04-24-genai-mil-concept-seed.md, 2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md, 2026-04-25-genai-mil-user-workflows-and-trust-boundaries.md]

In practice, the story should be short enough to state quickly, but complete enough to answer the judge’s core questions: who is it for, what pain does it solve, what evidence supports it, and why is the prototype credible? Winning-style projects in the source material do this by pairing a serious problem framing with a bounded demo and a visible human-in-the-loop posture. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md, 2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md]
