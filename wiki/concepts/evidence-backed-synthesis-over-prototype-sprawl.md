# Evidence-backed synthesis over prototype sprawl

Evidence-backed synthesis over prototype sprawl is a working approach for turning hackathon discovery into a narrow, reusable spec rather than a broad set of disconnected prototypes. The emphasis is on compiling curated markdown notes into a wiki, querying that wiki for repeated pain points, workflows, artifacts, trust boundaries, and candidate wedges, and using the resulting evidence to sharpen a single reusable direction. It treats the current evidence base as early hypothesis scaffolding rather than validated SME signal. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]

The approach favors synthesis over automatic expansion. Outside or public research should not be automatic; it should be used only when a specific evidence gap is worth filling. Because the hackathon timeline is short and access to DoD-experienced participants may be uneven, discovery should be opportunistic and interview-efficient rather than oriented toward overbuilding before the problem is sufficiently scoped. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]

In practice, the workflow begins with capturing raw observations, screenshots, voice notes, and interview notes in Slack or local private inboxes, then promoting that material into short, structured, redacted curated notes before ingest. The compiled wiki becomes a working-memory layer for repeated synthesis, while public-safe summaries or spec fragments are exported only after sanitization. This supports [[curated notes]], [[working memory]], [[interview synthesis]], and [[public-safe export]]. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]

The related trust boundary is important: raw notes and working synthesis remain private by default, the compiled wiki is not a public source of truth, and any AI-generated spec should preserve human review and avoid inventing operational facts. This framing helps prevent prototype sprawl by keeping the project focused on evidence-backed narrowing instead of speculative feature growth. Related concepts include [[human review]], [[trust boundaries]], [[spec synthesis]], and [[sanitized exports]]. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]
