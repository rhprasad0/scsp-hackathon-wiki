# DoD Responsible AI guidance stack

The DoD Responsible AI guidance stack is the layered policy and guidance backdrop used to shape Department of Defense AI development and use. In the source material, it includes the DoD AI Ethical Principles, the RAI Strategy and Implementation Pathway, the CDAO RAI Toolkit, the CDAO GenAI Toolkit, and the AI Cybersecurity Risk Management Framework Tailoring Guide. Together, these artifacts establish the trust posture, governance expectations, and security constraints that AI projects are expected to follow. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

At the foundation are the DoD AI Ethical Principles, issued in February 2020: Responsible, Equitable, Traceable, Reliable, and Governable. The next layer is the RAI Strategy and Implementation Pathway, signed in June 2022, which defines 64 lines of effort to operationalize those principles. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

The CDAO RAI Toolkit, launched in November 2023, provides a voluntary process for aligning AI projects with responsible AI best practices. The CDAO GenAI Toolkit, released in December 2024, extends that approach for generative AI and operationalizes the “Guidelines & Guardrails for Governance of Generative AI” memo with 33 additional GenAI-specific tools, including a prescreening guide for whether GenAI is the right technology for a given use case. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

For security and deployment concerns, the stack also includes the AI Cybersecurity Risk Management Framework Tailoring Guide, issued by DoD CIO in 2025. The source describes it as a companion to the RAI Toolkit, focused on CNSSI 1253, Zero Trust, and supply-chain risk for AI systems. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

In the GenAI.mil context, this guidance stack matters because the hackathon track is framed as an administrative and back-office problem space rather than a kinetic one. The source material says such projects should remain in a draft or recommend posture, preserve human review, keep trust boundaries visible, and avoid any design that would let AI take a kinetic action. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

For practical project design, the stack reinforces patterns like [[AI-assisted spec with human review]], [[Citations and missing-field visibility]], [[Controlled Reference Retrieval]], and [[Human-in-the-Loop Review and Approval]]. It also aligns with a narrow, reviewable workflow wedge rather than broad autonomous assistance. ^[2026-04-24-genai-mil-concept-seed.md, 2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md, 2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]
