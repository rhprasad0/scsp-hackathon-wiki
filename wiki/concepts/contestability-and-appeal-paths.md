# Contestability and Appeal Paths

Contestability and appeal paths are workflow features that let affected people challenge, review, or escalate a consequential decision after an automated or semi-automated system has classified a case, routed it, or taken an action. In the source material, appeal paths are discussed as part of broader exception-queue systems: if a case is not escalated properly, the person with authority may never see it in time, and a final outcome may be reached without [[Meaningful Human Review]]. ^[2026-04-29-analog-domains-exception-queues.md]

A useful framing from the source material is that contestability is not just about whether an appeal option technically exists, but whether it is usable, timely, and capable of changing the outcome. In public benefits, this includes human appeal paths, override authority, and escalation of error patterns; in content moderation, it includes human intervention, the ability to express a point of view, and a usable appeals process; in payment review, it includes customer notification, support, and time-bounded investigation. ^[2026-04-29-analog-domains-exception-queues.md]

The source material identifies several failure modes where appeal paths exist in name but not in practice. These include `APPEAL_ONLY_REVIEW`, `DECORATIVE_HUMAN_REVIEW`, `FAILURE_TO_ESCALATE`, opaque denials, weak support, missing documentation for overrides, and processes that say a case was reviewed without any meaningful consideration of new evidence or context. ^[2026-04-29-analog-domains-exception-queues.md]

Across the analog domains, the recurring control problem is an [[exception queues|exception-queue]] problem: a system decides what is routine, sends selected cases to humans, and writes a rationale or record. Contestability matters because the final approve/deny outcome is often less important than whether the system recognized ambiguity early enough to route the case to someone with real authority. Related concepts include [[human-in-the-loop]], [[adjudication]], [[complaint handling]], [[risk-based review]], and [[workflow routing]]. ^[2026-04-29-analog-domains-exception-queues.md]
