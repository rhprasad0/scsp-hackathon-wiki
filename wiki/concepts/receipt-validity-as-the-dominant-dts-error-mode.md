# Receipt validity as the dominant DTS error mode

Receipt validity is the clearest dominant failure mode in public DTS voucher-review guidance and oversight material. DTMO states that more than 70% of improper DTS payments are due to inadequate or invalid receipts, and it identifies lodging, rental car, and airfare as the most common receipt-related problem areas. Public Navy, DTMO, and service-level checklist materials repeatedly require AOs to verify that receipts are attached, legible, itemized where required, and consistent with the claimed expense amount. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md]

The public record also shows that receipt problems are not just a documentation nuisance; they are tied to AO liability and voucher returns. DTMO guidance says AOs may be held pecuniarily liable for improper payments they approve, and official materials instruct AOs to return vouchers when required receipts are missing or not adequate or valid. This makes receipt validation a central control point in [[Back-office military workflow examples]] and a strong fit for [[AO helper as quality assurance manager]]. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md, 2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-research-synthesis.md]

The specific receipt patterns called out in the source material are consistent across documents. Examples include pre-checkout folios that do not show payment, receipts that show only total charges rather than daily itemization, screenshots or booking confirmations instead of actual vendor receipts, balances still due, and missing visibility of key information such as the traveler’s name. The Navy checklist also explicitly checks for final lodging receipts, commercial air e-ticket receipts, and final rental car receipts with actual return details and final charges. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md]

Oversight material suggests that receipt validity is the main error mode not only because receipts are missing, but because DTS review is a high-friction human workflow. GAO reported that approving officials could not always view receipts that travelers had uploaded in Trip Workbook, which contributed to vouchers being approved without required receipts. DTMO and service checklists therefore emphasize standardized review steps, because the AO is expected to detect receipt/documentation problems before approval. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md]

This error mode also aligns with broader DTS triage patterns. Public materials repeatedly pair receipt issues with amount mismatches, duplicate expenses, and missing or weak justification text, but the receipt problem appears most consistently and most strongly quantified. In practice, that means any [[AO Radar pre-approval triage queue]] or similar assistant should treat receipt presence and validity as the first-pass screen, while preserving human review and avoiding automated approval or entitlement decisions. ^[2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-public-research.md, 2026-04-25-dts-ao-radar-research-synthesis.md]
