# Task Force Lima to AI RCC transition

The Task Force Lima to AI RCC transition refers to the handoff from Task Force Lima, a CDAO standing task force chartered in August 2023, to the [[AI Rapid Capabilities Cell (AI RCC)]], which began in December 2024 and continues as the successor organization. Task Force Lima assessed generative AI and LLM use cases for the DoD, cataloguing roughly 180–230+ candidate use cases and organizing them into 15 areas spanning warfighting and enterprise management. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

The transition marked a shift from an initial assessment effort to an operational capability cell. AI RCC is described as jointly run by CDAO and the Defense Innovation Unit, later under R&E and the Pentagon CTO, and as taking over Lima’s 15-use-case map. Its stated enterprise-management priorities include financial systems, HR, enterprise logistics and supply chain, healthcare information management, legal analysis and compliance, procurement processes, and software development/cybersecurity. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

The public context frames this transition as part of the broader consolidation of DoD generative AI activity into a single enterprise platform and operating model. In that framing, Task Force Lima provided the use-case inventory and early direction, while AI RCC became the mechanism for pilots and prioritized implementation, including $100M in FY24/25 plus $35M for four “Frontier AI” 90-day pilots. ^[2026-04-25-genai-mil-public-context-and-links.md]

For the GenAI.mil hackathon context, the transition matters because the track is said to sit squarely in the AI RCC-aligned enterprise-management space. The source material suggests that submissions should not merely replicate access to a model gateway, but instead add a wedge such as retrieval grounding, structured output, evaluation, or an offline/deployment posture beyond the base platform.
