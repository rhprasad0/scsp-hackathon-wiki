# Line of accounting and funding-source ambiguity

Line of accounting (LOA) and funding-source ambiguity refers to cases where a DTS packet or related travel expense cannot clearly identify which account, funding source, or “pot of money” should pay for the charge. In practitioner observations, this shows up as a common kickback reason alongside missing receipts, and it is described as part of the broader problem of reconstructing a coherent financial story from messy transactions, incomplete paperwork, and policy constraints. ^[2026-04-25-dts-practitioner-response-batch-1.md]

The ambiguity is not just a clerical issue. The source material frames packet review as audit-like: each expense needs a category, amount, purpose, supporting evidence, and plausible authorization and funding alignment. When the LOA is unclear, the reviewer may not know whether the purchase was authorized, which account should cover it, or whether the transaction can be justified against the available evidence. ^[2026-04-25-dts-practitioner-response-batch-1.md]

Practitioner notes suggest several signals that make funding-source ambiguity more likely: high complexity, missing dates, overlapping or inconsistent details, strange numbers, multiple charges where one would normally be expected, cash or ATM activity, exchange-rate math, and local or handwritten receipts. In these cases, the difficult part is not simply entering a form field; it is matching the transaction to an intelligible funding and evidence trail. ^[2026-04-25-dts-practitioner-response-batch-1.md]

For review workflows, an unclear LOA is a useful triage flag. The source material recommends that an AI assistant should highlight the ambiguity, package the evidence, identify missing or inconsistent information, and suggest reviewer questions or return-comment language, while leaving approval, certification, and entitlement decisions to the human AO or reviewer. Related concepts include [[AO checklist-driven voucher review]], [[AI-assisted spec with human review]], and [[Controlled Reference Retrieval]]. ^[2026-04-25-dts-practitioner-response-batch-1.md]
