# Interview-driven validation loop

The interview-driven validation loop is a discovery and synthesis pattern in which raw observations, screenshots, voice notes, and interview notes are captured privately, promoted into short structured curated notes, ingested into a compiled wiki, and then queried for repeated pain points, workflows, artifacts, trust boundaries, and candidate wedges. The loop is used to turn SME conversations and field notes into a narrow, reusable spec rather than a broad prototype, while treating the early evidence base as hypothesis scaffolding rather than validated SME signal. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]

In this pattern, discovery is intentionally opportunistic and interview-efficient because access to DoD-experienced participants may be uneven during a short hackathon. Outside or public research is not automatic; it is used only when a specific evidence gap is worth filling. The process therefore centers on repeated conversations, careful note promotion, and synthesis until the story stops changing and starts only sharpening. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]

The loop supports a private working-memory boundary rather than a public source of truth. Raw notes and working synthesis stay private by default, while downstream exports must be public-safe, sanitized, and billboard-safe. Any AI-generated spec should preserve human review and avoid inventing operational facts. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]

A practical output of the loop is a narrow, evidence-backed workflow wedge that can later be paired with controlled retrieval, structured drafting, visible review status, and reviewable outputs. In that sense, the interview-driven validation loop is a discovery method that helps produce [[AI-assisted spec with human review]], [[Scoped Prototype Wedge]], and [[Current-State Workflow Interviewing]]-style artifacts. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md, 2026-04-24-genai-mil-concept-seed.md, 2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]
