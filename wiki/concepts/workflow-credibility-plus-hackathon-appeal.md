# Workflow credibility plus hackathon appeal

“Workflow credibility plus hackathon appeal” is a framing principle for hackathon projects that need to be both operationally believable and engaging to judges. In the source material, the core idea is to keep a grounded, real workflow as the foundation while adding a visible technical wedge that makes the project creative, interesting, and technically challenging. ^[2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md]

The credibility side comes from a narrow, evidence-backed workflow with clear trust boundaries: a specific user persona, a bounded end-to-end task, cited sources, visible uncertainty, and human review before approval or export. This keeps the project aligned with public-safe, reviewable, administrative use cases rather than broad or speculative automation. Related concepts include [[AI-assisted spec with human review]], [[Controlled Reference Retrieval]], and [[Citations and missing-field visibility]]. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md, 2026-04-24-genai-mil-concept-seed.md, 2026-04-25-genai-mil-user-workflows-and-trust-boundaries.md]

The hackathon appeal side comes from making the mechanism easy to understand in a demo: a memorable technical hook such as retrieval, structured drafting, self-critique, evaluation, provenance tracking, or trust-boundary visualization. The organizer guidance explicitly warns against a submission that is only a sober workflow/spec artifact, and instead recommends a “boring workflow, spicy mechanism” shape. ^[2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md]

In practice, this balance means avoiding two failure modes: a flashy toy with weak workflow evidence, or a rigorous but dull spec that lacks a strong judging story. The suggested approach is to preserve the discovery/spec layer for grounding, then layer in a technically challenging mechanism that stays within the same bounded use case. Related concepts include [[Boring workflow, spicy mechanism]], [[Scoped Prototype Wedge]], and [[Creative but bounded framing]]. ^[2026-04-25-organizer-judging-signal.md]
