# Evidence-first spec synthesis

Evidence-first spec synthesis is a workflow for turning raw observations, interviews, screenshots, voice notes, and other field material into a narrow reusable spec by first building a private evidence base and then synthesizing from it. The process treats the early evidence as hypothesis scaffolding rather than validated SME signal, avoids automatic outside research unless there is a clear evidence gap, and favors a strong reusable spec over broad prototype sprawl. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]

The synthesis workflow starts with capturing raw material in private surfaces, promoting it into short structured redacted curated notes, ingesting those notes into a compiled wiki, and then querying for repeated pain points, workflows, artifacts, trust boundaries, and candidate wedges. The compiled wiki serves as working memory rather than a public source of truth, and downstream exports are limited to public-safe summaries or spec fragments. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]

A key feature of evidence-first spec synthesis is that the draft remains evidence-backed and reviewable. Any AI-generated spec should preserve human review, avoid inventing operational facts, and keep public-facing outputs sanitized; the resulting material should show sources, assumptions, unresolved fields, uncertainty, and review status before export. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md, 2026-04-24-genai-mil-concept-seed.md]

This approach is well suited to short hackathon discovery, where access to subject-matter experts may be uneven and interviews must be opportunistic and efficient. It encourages teams to use the early evidence base to identify a concrete workflow wedge, then tighten the concept as new interviews stop changing the story and only sharpen it. Related concepts include [[AI-assisted spec with human review]], [[Scoped Prototype Wedge]], [[Controlled Reference Retrieval]], [[Citations and missing-field visibility]], and [[Current-State Workflow Interviewing]]. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md, 2026-04-24-sme-discovery-plan.md]
