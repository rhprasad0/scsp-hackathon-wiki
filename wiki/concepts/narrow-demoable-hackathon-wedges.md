# Narrow, Demoable Hackathon Wedges

Narrow, demoable hackathon wedges are tightly scoped project directions that make a specific workflow or problem legible in a short demo. The source material suggests that strong hackathon projects are narrower, sharper, and more demoable than broad prototype sprawl, and that the best concepts are expressible as a single sentence that names the user, the artifact, the trusted references, the missing fields or assumptions, and who approves the output. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md]

This approach favors a single operational wedge over a general-purpose assistant or “dashboard + agents + map/chat/upload” toolbox. In the described hackathon context, a good wedge is bounded enough to be memorable, but still tied to a real workflow, protected asset, or concrete risk frame so that judges can quickly understand what changed for the user. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md]

The same pattern appears in the discovery and drafting workflow: discovery should be opportunistic and interview-efficient, with the early evidence treated as hypothesis scaffolding rather than validated SME signal. Curated notes are then compiled into a private working-memory wiki, queried for repeated pain points and candidate wedges, and used to sharpen one reusable direction rather than expand into multiple disconnected prototypes. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]

A narrow wedge is also designed to preserve trust boundaries. The underlying workflow emphasizes human review, visible uncertainty, and reviewable output: assistants may draft, cite, and structure content, but they must not invent policy, approve, autonomously submit, or bypass review. Users should be able to see sources, assumptions, unresolved fields, and review status before anything leaves the tool. ^[2026-04-24-genai-mil-concept-seed.md, 2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]

In practice, a demoable wedge often looks like a focused workflow assistant rather than a broad AI platform. Examples in the source material include diagnostic or defense-oriented prototypes, as well as a low-side drafting assistant that turns messy logistics or admin requests into cited, reviewable drafts with missing-field flags and human approval preserved. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md, 2026-04-24-genai-mil-concept-seed.md]
