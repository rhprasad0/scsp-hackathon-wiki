# Narrow, demoable hackathon wedges

Narrow, demoable hackathon wedges are tightly scoped project directions that make a specific workflow, risk, or artifact legible in a short demo. The source material suggests that strong hackathon projects are narrower, sharper, and more demoable than broad prototype sprawl, and that the best concepts can be expressed as a single sentence naming the user, the artifact, the trusted references, the missing fields or assumptions, and who approves the output. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md]

A good wedge is bounded enough to be memorable, but still tied to a real workflow, protected asset, or concrete risk frame so judges can quickly understand what changed for the user. The material contrasts this with “dashboard + agents + map/chat/upload” toolboxes that become too broad to read as a single operational idea. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md]

The discovery process is meant to stay opportunistic and interview-efficient. Early evidence is treated as hypothesis scaffolding rather than validated SME signal, then compiled into a private working-memory wiki that can be queried for repeated pain points and candidate wedges before narrowing to one reusable direction. ^[2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]

A narrow wedge also preserves trust boundaries. The workflow emphasized in the source material requires human review, visible uncertainty, and reviewable output: assistants may draft, cite, and structure content, but must not invent policy, approve, autonomously submit, or bypass review. Users should be able to see sources, assumptions, unresolved fields, and review status before anything leaves the tool. ^[2026-04-24-genai-mil-concept-seed.md, 2026-04-24-hackathon-operating-context.md]

In practice, a demoable wedge often looks like a focused workflow assistant rather than a broad AI platform. Examples include diagnostic or defense-oriented prototypes, and a low-side drafting assistant that turns messy logistics or admin requests into cited, reviewable drafts with missing-field flags and human approval preserved. ^[2026-04-24-prior-scsp-winning-project-observations.md, 2026-04-24-genai-mil-concept-seed.md]
